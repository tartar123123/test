What makes Villa Vento worthy of an award

Villa Vento (‘wind’ in Italian) in Sentosa, Singapore defies the monotone box-like form. Set against a backdrop of the marina, it has a façade that exudes power. It was inspired by the iconic C42 cabin cruiser, and thus conjures the image of a boat driving on water. There are glazed pearl white walls with a metallic tinge that replicate a hull. Structural elements were assembled with an orthogonal ground floor framework thrusting towards the canal. The upper level, meanwhile, boasts a vaulted roof.

It takes a specialised skill to build slanted structures, walls and glass, in order to execute the design intent. With its boat form, it fits perfectly in the environment as a part of it and not as a visual nuisance. It showcases how Mercurio Design Lab’s managing director Massimo Mercurio can create buildings that, quite literally, go beyond the box. Read more

ID&A’s unique judging process

What makes the International Design & Architecture Awards unique is its judging process. The decision is not made by a panel, but by more than a thousand industry professionals, design et al readers, clients and customers, who view the shortlisted entries online. It is, according to design et al publisher Joanne Beedles, “an incredible way to promote [one’s work], reach a targeted global audience, and ultimately sell [their] work.”
