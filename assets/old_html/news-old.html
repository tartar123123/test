The 2018 We Built

When three young Italian architects founded the studio MMV in 1967, they unknowingly established what would become one of the largest architectural consortia in Europe. More than fifty years later, one of its original founders, Dr. Alfonso Mercurio continues this savoir-faire through AMA Group, which itself continuously grows across Asia-Pacific through A.M. Associates. Its spin-off, Mercurio Design Lab, which focuses on avant-garde design, is led by his son, CEO and creative director Massimo Mercurio.

For the younger Mercurio, who hails from the Eternal City of Rome and was surrounded by soul-stirring art, grand fountains, and centuries-old palaces for over two decades, a visit to the high-octane Asia—with its glittering skyline and vibrant melting pot—would become a life-changing moment that made him realise the vast potentials of Italian design beyond Italy.

In 2018, ten years since Mercurio Design Lab’s establishment, the studio worked on a total of eight projects in eight different cities.

Among those that are being constructed are Wintastar Shanghai in China, a ski resort with integrated sports, entertainment, leisure and travel features. Set to be the world’s largest indoor ski resort to open in Shanghai, it made headlines in British broadsheet The Telegraph, which wrote: Behind the space-age facade of the building [ the interior draws] inspiration from European ski resorts, icebergs and the charm of the alps.”

Wintastar Shanghai, China

Meanwhile in Singapore, Dusit Thani Laguna, a hospitality facility with golf club, food and beverage, and retail sections is set to turn heads. The sleek, curvilinear form of Dusit Thani Laguna, formerly Laguna Golf and Country Club, soars over a six-storey hotel and a three-storey redesigned clubhouse. Surrounded by clusters of villas and inspired by the aerodynamic swing of the golf club, the two are linked by a circular central volume accessed by a ramp and a canopy for the pre-existing carpark. A shared, glazed façade not only envelops them but becomes their body.

Dusit Thani Laguna, Singapore

Doji Tower, Vietnam

The studio also worked on the interior design of Doji Tower in Vietnam. Fusing modern and classical elements, the spacious interiors will house offices, corporate lounges, meeting rooms, restaurants, an art gallery, and boutiques purveying gold jewelry, classic jewelry and watches. In here, the material palette includes marble tiles, metallic paints, and special finishings in champagne and gold.

Indonesia-1, Indonesia

Finally, the much-awaited Indonesia-1 Tower, with mixed-retail, office and serviced apartments, will soon soar over the Indonesian capital. Set to be the tallest twin towers in Jakarta, Indonesia-1 has a jewel-like quality with its grey stone base, copper tinted glazed facade and two cubes which form boldly decorative elements at the top of the building. The detailing adds character to the building with rounded corner windows and edges harking to the typical mosque form, thus lending to it an Islamic quality.

In addition, Mercurio Design Lab received six prestigious recognitions. Silver A’ Design Awards were given to Amber Skye for the Architecture, Building and Structure Design Category - Medium Rise Residential; as well as the Cultural Exchange Center, for the Architecture, Building and Structure Design Category - Convention Centre. Amber Skye was also featured in the International Property & Travel magazine as the award winner for Residential High-Rise Architecture Singapore of Asia Pacific Property Awards 2018-2019.

A Golden A’ Design Award, on the other hand, was handed to Mercurio Design Lab for Superstring at Hamilton Scotts. Villa Vento, meanwhile, was shortlisted for design et al’s International Architecture and Awards 2018 competition.

Finally, our creative director Massimo Mercurio moved to #31 in DAC’s World’s Leading Designers ranking from last year’s #46.

Under design director Kimberly Liu’s tutelage, Mercurio Design Lab has a global team of architects, engineers, designers and artists. The year 2018 also saw the opening of a thirty-strong Manila office, A.M. Associates (Philippines). In addition, new offices were registered in Shanghai, China, as well as Hanoi, Vietnam all within the same year. Together, these offices compose one brave soul to conquer the world.
