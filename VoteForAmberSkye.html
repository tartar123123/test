What makes Amber Skye worthy of an award

Masterfully crafted to resemble the sea’s infinite waves, Amber Skye, the 22- storey multi-residential development is stunning in a timeless sense. Located on the east coast of Singapore, the close vicinity to the seashore provided the best of all possible sources of inspiration: the sea and its endless waves in motion.

The dynamic force of the oceanic water is a very intriguing theme to render into an architectural expression. The major objective is to create an urban resort, offering the owners a feel of being sheltered in an oasis within the urban chaos, hence, the structure shows the design intent with an exceptional clarity – rounded balconies to represent sea waves.

The balcony’s edgings which curve continuously outwards and inwards are a masterwork done by the prefabricator, and obviously the most important feature of the building. The thickness and the fullness of these balconies are very essential as they are the strong elements of the building and not just a dubious decoration. The contractor did a magnificent work with the many unusually shaped elements making the building unique and a benchmark today for building organic architecture. With Amber Skye’s unique fluid structure, it is an inspiring icon that stands out from the rest, differentiating itself with a classic Italian architectural design that never goes out of style. ID&A’s unique judging process

What makes the International Design & Architecture Awards unique is its judging process. The decision is not made by a panel, but by more than a thousand industry professionals, design et al readers, clients and customers, who view the shortlisted entries online. It is, according to design et al publisher Joanne Beedles, “an incredible way to promote [one’s work], reach a targeted global audience, and ultimately sell [their] work.”
